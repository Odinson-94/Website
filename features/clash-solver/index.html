Connect to your ACC project. The AI pulls the latest clash report, deduplicates against the previous run, and separates the signal from the noise — model-link drift, family swaps, view-filter mismatches are flagged as ghosts, not real clashes.

For each real clash the AI proposes a resolution — re-route the smaller service, raise the ceiling void, swap a fitting — and shows you what the model would look like after.

The AI doesn't just report. For every real clash it proposes a concrete resolution: re-route, raise, swap, offset. Each proposal includes a model preview showing the result. You accept, refine or reject — one at a time or in zone-level batches.

Every accepted resolution writes to the model as a single Revit transaction — fully reversible, fully auditable. Rejected proposals go into a 'why we kept this' log so the next weekly sync doesn't waste your time proposing the same thing again.
